First Party Audits

A first-party audit, or internal audit, is a self-assessment conducted by own organization internally or by an expert on its product processes and systems. It ensures compliance with internal standards and regulations while identifying inefficiencies. By proactively discovering gaps before external reviews, companies can drive continuous improvement and maintain high-quality operational integrity.

Second Party Audits

These audits are performed by an organization on its suppliers or subcontractors to verify compliance with contractual requirements and safety standards. By assessing a supplier's quality and safety systems firsthand, companies can mitigate supply chain risks, ensure raw material consistency, and build stronger, more transparent commercial partnerships.

Gap Audits on International Standards

A gap audit acts as a pre-assessment tool to identify deficiencies between current operations and the requirements of standards like ISO 22000, FSSC 22000, BRCGS, EIC, AIB, etc. This systematic review highlights "gaps" in documentation and implementation, providing a clear roadmap for improvement and ensuring the organization is fully prepared for a formal certification audit.

Customized Audits to Develop Consolidated Standards

These audits involve merging multiple regulatory, international, and internal requirements into a single, unified assessment framework. By consolidating diverse standards—such as FSSAI, ISO, and specific client mandates—businesses can eliminate redundant checks, streamline compliance efforts, and maintain a high-performance benchmark tailored specifically to their unique operational needs.
