FoSTaC Training

Food Safety Training and Certification (FoSTaC) is a large-scale initiative by FSSAI aimed at ensuring that every food business has at least one trained and certified Food Safety Supervisor (FSS) for every 25 handlers. These training programs are tailored to specific industry sectors—such as catering, manufacturing, or retail—and cover best practices in hygiene, sanitation, and hazard management. By educating personnel on the ground, FoSTaC shifts the focus from mere reactive compliance to a proactive culture of food safety, significantly reducing the likelihood of foodborne illnesses and regulatory violations.

Artwork and Label Verification

Artwork and Label Verification involves a meticulous review of packaging elements, including the list of ingredients, nutritional facts, allergen warnings, veg/non-veg logos, and the placement of the FSSAI logo. This service ensures that all mandatory declarations are present and technically accurate according to the Food Safety and Standards (Labelling and Display) Regulations. Proper verification prevents costly product recalls, legal penalties, and the rejection of batches during market surveillance or regulatory inspections.

FSSAI Regulatory Trainings

Our expert-led training programs about FSSAI Regulations provide a deep understanding about various regulatory framework, including licensing, labelling and display, packaging, advertising and claims as well as licensing and registration. We empower your team with the technical knowledge needed to get a solid grasp of various requirements applicable to all Food Business Operators.

USFDA Registration

For food, beverage, and dietary supplement companies looking to enter the United States market, USFDA Registration is mandatory under the Bioterrorism Act. This process involves registering the facility with the U.S. Food and Drug Administration and, for foreign entities, appointing a U.S. Agent as a local point of contact. Registration ensures that the FDA can track facilities and notify them of inspections or safety concerns. Beyond the legal mandate, it signifies that the facility is prepared to meet stringent American safety standards, which is vital for passing U.S. Customs and Border Protection checks.
